Projects

Quad Assembly Line Design for Giant Motors Latinoamérica 2022

An assembly quad line design was required by Giant Motors Latinoamérica (GML), which had expected to start with assembly production of Italika quad on the next year. Also, the main requirement established was that some type of automation must be included within design. This project started with detailed research on assembly process, needed tools, equipment and industrial operators. Then the stages and duties were specified, and line was balanced depending on time calculations. Moreover, some type of automation solutions were explored, for example, collaborative robots, but finally, the team understood that data collecting would be the perfect grade of automation, that the enterprise would be interested in. The automated data collection system consisted of development a design and prototype of a time tracking system within a quad’s assembly line. The prototype included a RFID system, microcontrollers that make queries to a database, whose information was shown in a website app for real-time process tracking.

Responsabilities

  • Research on processes and tools to install suspention system
  • Development of data automation system: database design and connection, microcontroller programming, web design.

Prototype desing and manufacturing of electric wheelchair 2021

Partner enterprise: CRIT: Centro de Rehabilitación e Inclusión Infantil Teletón Puebla

CRIT is an organization that helps children with physical disabilities, whose physical mobility is affected by any type of illness. The organization lends wheelchairs to families, who can not afford one, but children are so weak to pull wheels on their own. That is why they always need babysitter supervision. Then the main goal of this project was set to give these children autonomy. The organization required us to design and build a prototype of electric wheelchair whit the following requirements:

  • Design of power transmission system that allows move wheels with DC motor rotation
  • Modular system without making modification to the wheelchair-basis
  • Speed and direction controlled by a Joystick
  • Low-cost design
At the end the prototype was delivered, which consisted of a modular platform that supports motors and batteries, also, a power transmission system that consists of sprockets, chain and a manufactured wheel camp, finally a electronic circuit and microcontroller that controlled speed and direction.

Responsabilities:

  • Power transmission CAD
  • Platform manufacturing. Welding and angle grinder.
  • Speed control design and programming.
